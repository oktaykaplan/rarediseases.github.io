Welcome to Kaplan's Lab

Kaplan Lab investigates the fundamental mechanisms of cilia, their role in genetic disorders, and their impact on cellular processes. Our work combines genetics, microscopy, and molecular biology to uncover how defects in cilia contribute to ciliopathies.

Our team is dedicated to advancing the understanding of cilia-related diseases and developing potential therapeutic strategies.

Research

Cilia are microtubule-based organelles critical for cellular signaling and movement. Dysfunction in cilia leads to a group of genetic disorders known as ciliopathies, affecting the kidney, brain, and other organs.

Our lab focuses on:

  • The role of ciliary proteins in development and disease
  • Cilia-mitochondria interactions and energy regulation
  • High-resolution imaging of cilia function
  • Genetic screening for novel ciliopathy-associated genes

We utilize model organisms like C. elegans and patient-derived cells to study these processes at a molecular level.
